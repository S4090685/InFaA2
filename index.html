Draft Concept

Theme:

Various environments and weather conditions.

Intended Audience:

People who enjoy listening to natural or ambient sounds while studying.

Technical Approach:

Interaction through keyboard or buttons, with buttons changing visually on hover.

Sound Ideas:

Nature and environmental sounds.

Video Ideas:

Animated visuals that correspond to different environments.

Project Title: To Study Space

Overview(concept): A self-study website that allows users to study immersively, with layered audio, background videos, and a visual timer. Users can mix ambient sounds and music using sliders and mute buttons. The website also displays the current date and time with an analog clock, providing real-time feedback. A countdown timer promotes focused study, giving both visual and audio alert cues when the time is up. Background videos can be easily switched via a panel, enhancing the immersive study environment. The overall design uses the principles of no-text-characters, skeuomorphic elements, and intuitive controls to make the experience both engaging and immersive.

Technical Approach

The project is developed primarily with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, creating a functional interactive prototype hosted on GitHub and deployed via Netlify. I have also included media assets (MP3 and MP4 files), which are major components for creating the immersive environment.

The main features of my project include:

  • An analog clock and timer.
  • A sound mixer with individual sliders.
  • A background video panel.

My strategy emphasizes a layered UI approach: the background video and sound controls capture the user‚Äôs attention, while the analog clock and timer provide supporting information. Together, these elements form a cohesive visual hierarchy that enhances usability and immersion.

No Text Characters

I tried to achieve a text-free interface by using icons and images to show users what each button represents. This was challenging because some concepts do not have a specific icon that directly represents them. To solve this, I used related icons as substitutes. For example, I replaced the text for ‚Äúlibrary/study space‚Äù with a pencil or a book, and ‚Äúliving room‚Äù with a couch.

Skeuomorphism

Skeuomorphism is a design style in which digital user interfaces mimic real-world objects and their characteristics. In my project, this is shown through the analog clock. The clock includes hour, minute, and second hands that rotate in real time, and it uses shadowing to create a 3D effect, making it resemble a physical clock. By bringing a real-world object into the interface, users can instantly recognize and read the current time without leaving the webpage.

Another skeuomorphic element I implemented is the old-style television. It has buttons that allow users to switch between different channels. Clicking these buttons in the interface is similar to how people used to change channels on physical televisions. This design references the way people interacted with television sets in the past, while digitizing the experience without the need for physical controls.

The volume slider also mimics audio equipment, such as mixing consoles and DJ controllers. Sliding it to the left lowers the volume, while sliding it to the right increases it. This mirrors the physical interaction users would expect from real-world sound equipment.

References

My noise

https://mynoise.net/NoiseMachines/jungleNoiseGenerator.php/

I looked through MyNoise Jungle Life, which contains a series of sounds from a jungle environment. The sounds are all controlled with a play button and start and stop at the same time. Users can only adjust the volume range, with each slider representing a different sound classified as Bass, Mids, and Treble. This influenced my design choice of using sliders for different sounds; however, in my version, each sound can be muted individually. I chose to reference MyNoise because I was drawn to the feature that allows each ambient sound to be adjusted independently, creating an immersive experience that directly reflects the project‚Äôs title.

Defonic

https://defonic.com/

I also looked at Defonic ‚Äì Create Immersive Soundscapes. The layout of the website is not immersive but deliberately simple, avoiding information overload. It is clear how the website functions and it is easy to navigate. The use of environmental ambient sound inspired me and informed how I decided to incorporate sounds into my own project. On Defonic, the sounds can layer over each other depending on user choice. However, I wanted my project to be more immersive, so I carefully explored looping videos and sounds that match with each other. In my project, the visual and audio systems respond to one another, creating a stronger connection between visuals and sound.

Forest

https://www.forestapp.cc/

This is an application where you simply plant a tree and wait until the timer finishes before using your phone; otherwise, the tree will die. Forest uses the technique of loss aversion to keep users focused, reflecting the idea that people prefer to avoid loss rather than acquire equivalent gains. The app also includes a system where the more time you stay focused, the bigger the tree grows. Users can choose longer sessions to grow larger and more visually appealing trees.

This concept inspired me to create a study space website that encourages users to remain within the interface and study. For my project, I incorporated visual looped videos and ambient sounds to create an immersive environment. While I liked the idea of planting trees, I did not want to restrict users. Instead, I implemented a timer to allow them to track their own study sessions, along with an analog clock so they can check the time without frequently switching devices or touching the screen.

Viisual Plan

  • Do you find Skeuomorphism in this prototype?
Out of 9 responses, 5/9 noted that they found skeuomorphism in my project, mainly reflected in the analog clock and the background video. This aligns with my intention to demonstrate skeuomorphism. The sounds I implemented on the website also enhance this effect, including tracks of people talking, rain, birds, etc.
  • Did you find anything confusing?
Most respondents mentioned that there was no sound in that version. I had indeed forgotten to include all the sound tracks. There were helpful suggestions, such as having a default sound playing when users enter the website so they can immediately understand the sound panels at the bottom. The mute and unmute buttons were also noted as quite small, and I will resize them to improve usability.
  • Is the website easy to navigate?
Most respondents agreed with the overall design, though one feedback point stood out: the suggestion to add a play button. I hadn‚Äôt considered a play button since users can layer multiple sounds and play whichever they like. However, I realized it would improve the user experience to have a tool to pause all sounds easily.
  • How did you feel while using this website?
Again, most people were confused by the lack of sound, but one person correctly identified the project‚Äôs purpose to provide calming background audio for studying.
  • If you could add anything to the page, what would you add?
Overall, most of the feedback was helpful, and I plan to implement suggestions such as adding a pop-up activation, adjusting the layout, including the date, and resizing the mute/play buttons. I will test these elements to see how they fit within the project.
